About Us

Our responsibility is to ensure every child has great teachers and great school leaders. In the Vision Public Schools we tailor instruction to meet the individual needs of every student. Together, we are:

1. Strengthening teaching and school leadership

2. Replicating success and turning around low- performing schools

3. Deepening partnerships with parents, students, and the community

4. Redesigning district services for effectiveness, efficiency, and equity

Latest News

VISION PUBLIC SCHOOLS PARTNERS WITH MUSEUM OF FINE ARTS

Vision Public Schools (VPS) partnered with the Museum of Fine Arts, Utah (MFA) to celebrate the 10th anniversary of the Teen Arts Council to present HOMiE:In Our Eyes. The exhibit made its debut this past Saturday, July 16, 2016

THREE VISION PUBLIC SCHOOLS TEACHERS NAMED TEACHER-RANGER-TEACHERS BY NATIONAL PARK SERVICE

The Vision Public Schools (VPS) is pleased to announce that three VPS teachers have been named Teacher-Ranger-Teachers by the National Park Service. As Teacher-Ranger-Teachers, Cindy Muenkel and Danielle Aucoin of Harvard-Kent Elementary School in Charlestown and Emma Fialka-Feldman from Dudley Street Neighborhood Charter School in Roxbury will spend six weeks this summer earning 6 graduate credits studying place-based education, and working with historic sites around Utah to develop teacher curricular resources for supporting site visits by Vision Public Schools students

VPS KICKS OFF FREE SUMMER MEAL PROGRAM FOR KIDS

VISION — Vision Public Schools’ (VPS) Food and Nutrition Services Department celebrated the beginning of the Summer Food Service Program on Tuesday, July 12, with a free barbecue kickoff event at the Joseph Lee K-8 School in Dorchester in partnership with Project Bread and various organizations
